Spencer Bell

Arkane Studios website

Hick's Law

Arkane studios' website exemplifies hicks law. Their page is layed out simply and doesn't present more than one set of options to the user at once. As you scroll down, you see the work for us page, starting first with the two work locations. If you're interested, you might choose the one you live closest too and go from there.

Bethesda website

White Space

Bethesda shows off great white space design. They give each game studio they advocate for space to breath and exist on the page. When you land there, it's all you see aside from the title and nav bar. It puts all the focus on these 6 items and gives you space to breath too.

Xbox Website

Visual heirarchy

Xbox's landing page is emblematic of good visual heirarchy. If I visit the xbox website, I see their rotating hero image first which is perhaps an ad for something I might be interested in(and the associated call to action button if I am), then I see what I came for which is probably to buy or look at something i might buy. Each of the 6 options for action has equal weight at the bottom of the page. It's easy to sort out visually what someone might be there to do or want on the page and what is most important.
